Crop Information

Cotton Wheat Rice

Cotton

Cotton is a soft, fluffy staple fiber that grows in a boll, or protective case, around the seeds of the cotton plants of the genus Gossypium in the mallow family Malvaceae. The fiber is almost pure cellulose.

Wheat

Wheat is a grass widely cultivated for its seed, a cereal grain which is a worldwide staple food. There are many different varieties of wheat, but the most widely grown is common wheat (Triticum aestivum).

Rice

Rice is a grain crop and the seed of the grass species Oryza sativa (Asian rice) or Oryza glaberrima (African rice). As a cereal grain, it is the most widely consumed staple food for a large part of the world's human population, especially in Asia.
